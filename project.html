Overview

My project idea is to create a mobile app.   The app is called "Cent Auction" and is a revenue generating business.

A "Cent Auction" or sometimes, "Penny Auction" is traditionally a charity based fundraising model where donated prizes are auctioned off.   These were quite popular when I was young and often used at things like school fete's. 

The general premise of the game is that you purchase "tickets" to use in the auction to win prizes.

An item comes up for auction and you submit an "entry" for the item using your tickets.   Whoever had the highest number of entries wins the prize item and committed tickets are collected from all participants.

 

Motivation

This project is very interesting to me as it's a business idea I have had for some time that I believe would be successful.  It doesn't seem like a terribly difficult thing to program and I think it would be very popular as you can win prizes for small amounts of money.   As the business and userbase grows, the quality or frequency of the auctions can change.

Mobile Gaming is a $68.5 billion dollar a year industry.1 

The Online Gambling industry is expected to reach $95 billion dollars a year by 2024.2

As the Cent Auction falls halfway between both gaming and gambling, there is ample opportunity there to capitalise on a huge and growing market.

 

Description

As the Cent Auction falls into an auction format, there are no age restrictions like there would be with an Online Gambling product.  This means the app is open to all ages and can easily get listing approval for the App store & Google Play.

The Cent Auction will start out with a number of smaller auctions to drive user growth.  Items with a value less than $100 so that a lot of people win these prizes and then are encouraged to keep participating and refer friends.

The auction format itself will be based on tickets, not dollars.   A user may purchase packs of tickets to use on the auction.  For example a spend of $5 will get you 50 tickets to use in auction and if you refer a friend that spends at least $5 you will also get a bonus 50 tickets.

The auction format itself will show the item for sale with a full description and will include a closing date for the auction.   A user of the app can choose to commit any number of tickets to the auction and the winner is the user that made the most entries while the Cent Auction collects all of the tickets the users committed.

For example, we might list a Sony Playstation 4 (PS4) with a real value of $400.   In the auction, there were five (5) participants, who each made the following entries:

Person A:   2000 tickets

Person B:   1500 tickets

Person C:   1500 tickets

Person D:   1000 tickets

Person E:    10 tickets

 

Person A has the highest entries so they get the PS4 even though they only paid an equivalent of $200 for a $400 system!

The Cent Auction then collects all the tickets which totals:   6010.   Or, the equivalent of $601 which pays for the cost of the prize, postage, hosting and the rest is profit.

After the auction closes, the participants are all notified what the winning number of entries was and given a first name and city for the winner. Ie.  "John from Brisbane, 2000 tickets."

The range of auction items offered can vary greatly, from PS4's to gift cards to holidays.  Once the userbase has grown enough, it would also be great to reach out to Australian businesses and get them to donate a prize for the auction.  This way you don't have the initial expense of purchasing the prize and the business gets a lot of advertising by way of exposure to the userbase.

One way to keep the auctions fun could be to have a flexible end time.   For example, as soon as the value of the prize plus 30% has been committed in tickets, the auction immediately switches over to last chance and finishes in 30 minutes, sending notifications to participants.

Participants also have the ability to top up their number of entries at any time.  If they initially use 100 tickets, they might want to add some more tickets to increase their entries after they receive the "Last Chance" 30 minute notification.

 

Tools and Technologies

The app will be built on both iOS and Android mobile platforms.  To build on iOS it is necessary to get Apple Xcode 11 for development.3 Also, adding in the Swift UI makes building the app much easier.3 

To develop an Android app, you need Android Studio.4  The code editor that comes with Android Studio provides code completion for Kotlin, Java, and C/C++ languages so it would be important to be fluent in at least one of these.  I have touched on Java previously and really liked it, so I would like to focus on this for app development.  

I have also found that there is a cross platform solution called the "Multi-OS Engine" which enables you to write the code once in Java and it will modify it to work on both iOS and Android platforms.5    This would be a great time saver and avoid having to build in Java for Android and then again in C for iOS.

 

Skills Required

Some of the skills and software required are mentioned above.    Apple Xcode 11 and Android studio.  iOS apps are written in Objective-C and Android apps can be written in multiple languages. 

It will be necessary to learn both Objective-C and Java to develop on both platforms without using a multi-platform tool as these can sometimes not be as good as developing in native languages.

A payment processor is another bit of software that needs to be added to the app, however this is fairly simple with easy ways to insert this code provided by the processor and does not require a lot of programming on my end.

 

Outcome

If the project is successful, it will result in a new, profitable business that I will be running. I will need to devote a lot of time to the business including, but not limited to:  sourcing new prizes, promotions, running the auction, fraud prevention and auction security.  Also, posting prizes to the winners.   In fact, there will be so much going on with a successful business that I will likely need to hire people to run these operations.

If successful locally in Australia, I will then develop versions of the app for different countries as the local business marketing aspect of it will need to be region specific.
