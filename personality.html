I completed three personality tests.   

The first is the Myers Briggs test and it returned my personality type as ISTP.1  

In short, an ISTP is introverted, sensing, thinking, perceiving.  People with ISTP personalities enjoy having time to think alone and are fiercely independent. ISTPs also love action, new experiences, hands-on activities and the freedom to work at their own pace.

 

The second is a Learning Styles test.2  

This test shows that I learn best through kinesthetic learning.  Kinesthetic style is learning by doing. You learn in this manner whenever you capture new information through the process of physical activity. It is often referred to as a hands-on learning experience.

Some characteristics of a kinesthetic learner are:

  • Good at learning through hands-on experience
  • Often bored with traditional textbook learning
  • Likes to move around and explore their environment
  • Usually enjoys athletics and physical education
  • Likes to touch objects and people
Lastly, I completed a DISC assessment.3  Based on the ideas of psychologists William Marston and Walter Clarke, it evaluates behaviour. It focuses on the traits of dominance, inducement, submission, and compliance (DISC). Some companies rely on it to hire staff, while others use it to gauge an employee's suitability for a job.

The test results show that I have a blend of both Dominant and Inspiring traits. My Dominant traits are probably a little stronger than my Inspiring traits. Some words that describe me are:

  • Directive
  • Decisive
  • Driven, and
  • Interactive

The results show I am task-oriented, and probably get a great deal done. It says I probably like problem solving and getting results. I am comfortable interacting with others to make things happen.

 

 

What do the results of these tests mean for me?

I think these results are an accurate reflection of my personality.   The tests serve to reinforce my own opinions of myself and my work / learning style.  I believe it also shows I am well suited to the Information Technology industry.

 

How do I think these results may influence my behaviour in a team?

These results will help me view my behaviour in a team objectively and correct my actions if I find I am becoming too dominating or decisive.   If I'm working in a team that I am not leading, I need to ensure I pull back a little and take a more cooperative role and engage with the other team members and their ideas more fully.

 

How should I take this into account when forming a team?

When forming a team, I believe it's important to have a good mix of members whose personalities and learning styles work well together.  If I'm forming a team, I should try to ensure my team members also learn primarily through kinesthetic or visual methods.  If I am not forming the team, I will need to ensure there are learning and planning methods that work well not only for me, but also for the other team members.
